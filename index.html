What is CMFA PressCon Tracker?

This tool is an interactive browser and analytics dashboard for the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs Press Conferences Corpus (CMFA PressCon v6) — the largest publicly available dataset of Chinese diplomatic discourse, covering 23 years of daily MFA press briefings.

It was built to help researchers, journalists, and students explore patterns in how Beijing frames foreign policy positions, responds to international scrutiny, and shifts its diplomatic register across political eras — without requiring any programming knowledge.

What can you do here?

🔍
Browse & Search
Full-text search across 35,346 Q&A pairs. Filter by era, spokesperson, topic, media outlet, sentiment and more.
📊
Analyse Trends
Sentiment over time, topic prevalence, media hostility matrix, Q–A correlation, era word profiles and more.
📋
Export & Cite
Export any filtered subset as CSV. Generate Chicago, APA, BibTeX citations for individual entries.
📝
Write Methods
One-click methodology paragraph describing your exact filter selection — ready to paste into a paper.
💡 This tracker is a visualisation layer on top of the dataset. The underlying data, sentiment scores, and named entities were produced by Mochtak & Turcsanyi (2021) and subsequent NLP processing — not by this interface.

The Dataset

The corpus was built from official transcripts of Chinese MFA press conferences — a unique daily format where Chinese and foreign journalists ask questions on any topic and a spokesperson responds on behalf of the government.

Political Eras

The dataset spans four distinct leadership periods, each with a different diplomatic style:

Hu Jintao Era2002–2012 · "Harmonious Society" · 9,736 entries · Restrained, multilateral, relationship-focused Early Xi Era2013–2017 · "Chinese Dream" · 6,480 entries · More assertive on territorial issues, AIIB, BRI Wolf Warrior Era2018–2022 · Trade War + COVID · 12,231 entries · Sharply confrontational, lowest avg sentiment Post-Pandemic Era2023–2025 · Re-engagement · 6,899 entries · Partial diplomatic normalization

Understanding Sentiment Scores

Every question and answer in the dataset has been scored using XLM-R-ParlaSent (classla/xlm-r-parlasent), a multilingual transformer model trained on parliamentary speech. The scale runs from 0 to 5.

Very neg.
1–2
Negative
2–3
Neutral
3–4
Positive
4–5
Very pos.
Avg Q sentiment2.18 — press questions trend slightly negative, reflecting journalistic challenge Avg A sentiment3.08 — spokesperson answers are typically more positive than the questions asked Q–A gap~0.90 on average. A shrinking gap signals diplomatic tension (Wolf Warrior era gap: 0.63)

The Q–A Gap as an Analytical Tool

The difference between question sentiment and answer sentiment is one of the most analytically useful signals in the dataset. A large gap (assertive answers to hostile questions) characterises confident, proactive diplomacy. A small gap (answers converge with hostile question tone) signals a more defensive, embattled posture — the "Wolf Warrior" pattern.

Named Entity Recognition

Locations, persons, and organisations mentioned in each question and answer were extracted using Trankit and XLM-RoBERTa-base. These appear in the Country, Person, and Organisation filter panels and are displayed as coloured tags when you open an individual entry.

⚠️ Sentiment scores reflect the tone of the text, not factual content. A diplomat saying "China firmly opposes any interference" scores low even though it represents a confident position. Use scores as proxies for rhetorical register, not policy substance.

Filters Reference

Search

Full-text search matches your query against questions, answers, or both (toggle with the Q+A / Q only / A only selector). Search is case-insensitive. Matching text is highlighted in yellow in results.

Year Range & Political Era

Use the sliders or type directly into the year boxes. Clicking a Political Era button automatically sets the year range. You can also drag the Timeline Scrubber (the bar chart above results) to zoom into any period — double-click to reset.

Spokesperson

Check one or more spokespeople to filter results. Multiple selections show entries from any of the selected spokespeople. Counts next to each name reflect the total in the full dataset.

Media Origin

Filter by outlet type (Western Wire/Print, Chinese State Media, Asian/Regional, Unattributed). Unattributed covers entries before March 2020 when outlets were not recorded. Use Specific Media Outlet to select or search individual outlets.

Western Wire/PrintReuters, AFP, Bloomberg, BBC, CNN, NYT, AP, Financial Times, Guardian… Chinese State MediaCCTV, Xinhua, China Daily, Global Times, CRI, CGTN, People's Daily… Asian / RegionalNHK, Kyodo, Yonhap, PTI, TASS, RIA Novosti, Al Jazeera…

Country / Organisation / Person

These filters search the named-entity fields (extracted by NER, not full-text). Click quick-select chips or type a custom value. Only one country/org/person can be active at a time.

Sentiment Sliders

Fine-tune the exact Q or A sentiment range. Combined with the quick-select presets or used independently. Range is 0–5 in steps of 0.1.

💡 Use URL deep-linking — every filter combination is encoded in the URL. Copy Link button copies the current filter state as a shareable URL. Use Saved Presets to store named filter configurations locally.

Topics & Rhetorical Registers

Both topic themes and rhetorical registers are keyword-detected from the Q+A text at load time — they are not pre-labelled in the original dataset. Multiple themes or registers can match a single entry.

Analytics Dashboard Guide

The Analytics page (open ↗) contains 15 research visualisations built from pre-computed statistics across the full 35,346-entry corpus.

Activity ChartAnnual Q&A volume 2002–2025, annotated with geopolitical events. Red bars = Wolf Warrior era. Sentiment DynamicsMean Q and A sentiment per year + the Q–A gap chart + year-on-year volatility. The gap narrowed from ~1.5 (2010) to ~0.6 (2021). Q–A Scatter~4,400 sampled entries plotted by question vs answer sentiment, colour-coded by spokesperson. Regression line + r², p-value, and plain-English interpretation. Topic Frequency% of entries per year mentioning each of the 13 themes. Use the group buttons to focus. In 2020, Health/Pandemic reached 49.6% — the highest single-theme concentration. GeographyAnnual mention counts for 10 countries/regions in spokesperson answers. Toggle trend lines per country. SpokespeopleAvg answer sentiment and Q–A gap per spokesperson. Zhao Lijian (2.74 avg A) vs Jiang Yu (3.42 avg A) show the widest range. Hostility Matrix2D bubble scatter: X = avg Q sentiment (outlet hostility), Y = avg A sentiment received. BBC and Xinhua define the two extremes. Media RankingsBar chart of avg Q sentiment by outlet. BBC (1.49) vs Xinhua (2.78) = 1.29-point gap across hundreds of questions each. KWIC CollocationEnter any keyword to find its 20 most frequent neighbours in MFA answers. Reveals framing patterns. Requires dataset to be cached. Bilateral ComparisonPick two countries to compare annual mention frequency in answers across all 23 years. Era Word ProfilesTF-IDF-scored word bubbles for each era's distinctive vocabulary. "Pompeo", "COVID", "Xinjiang" dominate 2018–22. Q&A LengthAverage character count per year. Answer length peaked at ~1,254 chars (2021) — verbosity as a proxy for narrative control. Head-to-HeadSelect any two spokespeople for a direct sentiment, gap, and entry-count comparison. Co-occurrence Matrix13×13 heatmap showing which topics appear together. New Features in This Version

🌙
Dark Mode
Toggle with the ☀️/🌙 button in the top-right. Your preference is saved and persists across sessions.
⭐
Bookmarks
Star any entry from the table or modal. Filter to bookmarked-only with the ⭐ button. Export bookmarks as a CSV from the modal.
💾
Filter Presets
Save any filter combination by name using the sidebar preset panel. Load, rename or delete presets at any time.
📊
Timeline Scrubber
Drag across the histogram above results to zoom into any year range. Double-click to reset to full 2002–2025.
📋
Citation Generator
Open any entry and click "Cite" to get Chicago, APA, BibTeX and a direct URL — all one-click copy.
📝
Methods Paragraph
Top-bar "Methods Note" button generates a full academic paragraph describing your current filter state. Paste directly into your paper.
▾
Extended Stats
Click "▾ more" in the stats bar to reveal std deviation, median Q/A sentiment, the Q–A gap, and top spokesperson for your current filter.
🔗
Deep Linking
Every filter state is encoded in the URL. "Copy Link" copies the full shareable URL. Paste it in a paper, email, or browser to restore your exact view.
