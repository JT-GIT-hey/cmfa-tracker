Rhetorical Shift Post-2019
Answer sentiment dropped from 3.4 (2011–18 avg) to 2.8 (2019–22) — China's diplomatic tone became measurably more defensive during trade war escalation and COVID.
COVID Dominated 2020
In 2020, 49.6% of all press conference entries mentioned health/pandemic terms — the single highest theme concentration in the 23-year dataset.
Media Hostility Quantified
BBC ask the most adversarial questions (avg Q sentiment: 1.49). MFA responds most positively to Associated Press of Pakistan (4.35 avg A sentiment) — a 2.86 gap.
Q–A Correlation Weakens
The correlation between question hostility and answer defensiveness has weakened since 2019 — the MFA increasingly gives low-sentiment answers regardless of question tone.
Answer Length Doubled
Average answer length grew from ~569 chars (2002) to ~1,254 chars (2021), peaking during the Wolf Warrior era — a proxy for narrative control effort.
Wolf Warrior Lexicon
The 2018–2022 era is uniquely defined by words like "pompeo," "coronavirus," "vaccine," "xinjiang," "covid" — a vocabulary that didn't exist in prior MFA discourse.
