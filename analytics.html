Rhetorical Shift Post-2019
Answer sentiment dropped from 3.4 (2011–18 avg) to 2.8 (2019–22) — China's diplomatic tone became markedly more defensive following trade war escalation and COVID.
COVID Dominated 2020
In 2020, 49.6% of all press conference entries mentioned health/pandemic terms — the single highest theme concentration in the dataset's 23-year span.
Media Outlet Bias Gap
Western wire agencies (Reuters: 1.96, AFP: 1.95) ask measurably more negative questions than Chinese state outlets (CCTV: 2.69, China Daily: 2.39).
US Dominates Geographic Focus
The US appears in answer named-entity fields 3× more than the next most-cited country (Japan, 2,284). US-China mentions surged 40% between 2018–2020.
Answer Length Doubled
Average answer length grew from ~600 chars (2002) to ~1,100 chars (2025), suggesting increasingly elaborate official responses to press scrutiny.
Conflict Coverage Peaks
Military/conflict topics spiked to 37.5% in 2002 (post-9/11), peaked again at 32% in 2022 (Russia-Ukraine invasion), showing the corpus tracks global events.
